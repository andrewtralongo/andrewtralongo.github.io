Album cover design for The 1975's single UGH!, including a cassette, CD, vinyl cover and Spotify icon.

Design Rationale


Their new album I Like It When You Sleep, for You Are So Beautiful yet So Unaware of It is based on bold decisions, thus the title of the album, and is a reinvention of their image from monochromatic grunge with lyrics written from the gutter to a loud exaggeration of the music industry and fame, simultaneously battling the doubts which live within yourself. This feature single focus’s on the singer’s drug addiction to cocaine, stating “I’m not giving it up”em>. The lyrics describe his current lifestyle and the issues and conflict he faces in trying to give up the drug addiction.

The design reflects their stylistic revolution, the black and white photography reflecting their origins and prejudice ‘indie-rock’ appearance while the rest of the design represents their current loud ‘pink’ visual identity; I have directly appropriated the two pastel colours of their new aesthetic in the design. The photography represents a direct visual representation of the song: the hotel room floor after a long night of partying. Created out of hand-made type, each cover includes one character of the song’s title. The singer is known to smoke cigarettes and drink wine while performing thus the choice for their use in the image. The opposite side simply displaying UGH! is a juxtaposition to the complexity to the photography, and the band has stated that the song’s title is a ‘understated disappointment’ thus the reasoning to its simplicity. The vinyl slipcase repeats the lyric “I’m not giving it up”, reflecting the difficulty in battling an addiction.
