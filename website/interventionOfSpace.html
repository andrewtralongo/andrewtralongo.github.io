- Hunting + Gathering in the Digital Wilderness


Photobombing. The act of spoiling a photograph by unexpectedly appearing in the camera’s field of view as the picture is taken. Ever since 2009 this act has received global coverage and exposure, relative with the evolution of smartphones migrating into the everyday. While some may do this on purpose as a practical joke or even to make a social/political agenda, others may simply be caught in the background of an image.

This photographic archive is an exploration of an intervention of space, additionally questioning what ‘spoils’ an environment and whether it should be there at all.

// Andrew Tralongo
