Everyone in one way or another participates in the act of body alteration and decoration. From harmless ear piercings to neck tattoos, this art form has existed throughout civilisations and meanings can differ from aesthetic to religious symbolism. This publication explores the tattoo, jewellry, piercings and scars of people and the stores associated to them.

I decided to get my first tattoo a week before I got it in December of 2014. Now in almost three years,I have twelve. I’m constantly told that I will regret them, and who knows, they are probably right, but for me, my tattoos are a representative of who I am and who I was; they have become a part of me and my identity. I find it normal to look at my arm and see lines of morse code or an illustration of the mad hatter; I even often forget of the arrow on the back of my forearm because I usually only see it in a mirror.

I see the body as a canvas and as a designer, as an artist, I find it natural that the way I present myself carries meaning; from the fact that I don’t have any colour tattoos to the clothes I put on in the morning, it creates an aesthetic which reflects my personality. So it doesn’t matter if they have meaning or not, meaning can grow or change. I know that they will blur with age, but so what? It's not like your skin stays in prime condition anyway, so why not colour yourself in a bit?
